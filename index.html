Themeable App Alternatives

A list of alternatives to non-themeable apps

Home Manifesto Debunking Myths

We believe that the ability to theme your desktop and apps is one of the most important and valued features of Desktop Linux and one of the biggest selling points to persuade users to switch away from Windows and Mac.

People spend a lot of time using their computer and they want to make their apps and desktop look the way they like for the same reason that people like to decorate the interior of their homes and wear the clothes they like.

Some app authors have suggested that theming causes some problems but we don't believe that many of these arguments are valid. See Debunking arguments against theming.

To this end, here is a list of apps which are difficult or impossible to theme or do not follow your chosen desktop theme (mostly libadwaita) along with some good alternatives which follow your desktop theme:

    gnome-clocks
  • Hourglass - A GTK4 app that uses libgranite
  • Alarm Clock (applet) - A GTK3 app
    gnome-font-viewer
  • FontManager - A simple font management application for GTK Desktop Environments
